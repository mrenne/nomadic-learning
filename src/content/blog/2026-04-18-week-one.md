Welcome to the first entry of Nomadic Learning. The premise is simple: AI is reshaping what it means to acquire knowledge, and I want to think through that out loud — week by week.

Let's start with the uncomfortable one.

**If a student can ask an AI anything and get a reliable answer in seconds, what's the point of knowing things?**

The easy answer is "critical thinking." But that's a dodge — critical thinking still requires a base of knowledge to think *about*. You can't evaluate a claim about climate models without understanding what a model is. You can't spot a flawed historical analogy without knowing the history.

The harder answer: the value is shifting from *storing* knowledge to *navigating* it. Knowing what question to ask, being able to judge the quality of an answer, and connecting new information to existing mental models — these compound over time in ways that raw retrieval doesn't.

That's the skill worth building. Not memorization. Not just "prompting." Something in between that doesn't have a clean name yet.
