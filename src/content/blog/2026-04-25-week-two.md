---

This week I've been testing two AI tutoring tools side by side with a group of middle schoolers, and the difference in approach is striking.

**Khanmigo** leans Socratic — it refuses to just give answers, nudging students toward the reasoning themselves. Frustrating at first, but something interesting happens after about 20 minutes: kids start *arguing* with it. That's a good sign.

**Synthesis**, originally built for SpaceX kids, throws learners into collaborative simulations and games. Less tutoring, more *thinking under pressure*. Engagement is through the roof, but it's harder to measure what's sticking.

The deeper question: are we optimizing for the right thing? Fast answers feel like learning. Struggle *is* learning. The tools that understand that distinction will matter most.

## What I'm watching

- Whether schools adopt AI tutors as supplements or replacements
- How students describe the experience in their own words (spoiler: they don't say "it taught me")
- The emerging research on AI-assisted retrieval practice vs. passive Q&A
